Ticker: PCOR

Position: Long

Investment Thesis

  1. Comprehensive platform + industry consolidation create a durable advantage.

    Procore’s comprehensive suite of construction management software places it in a competitive position against other players. Given the company is targeting large enterprises with its pricing and offerings, the comprehensiveness of the software is a competitive advantage. As the construction industry is also consolidating, industry dynamics work strongly in Procore’s favor.

    1. Tegus: Comprehensive software seems to be a big plus for companies, but pricing is a concern for not only SMBs but also mid-sized businesses.
    2. Where it’s stickiest: Most sticky users are GCs; expansion to more owners and property management can be hard (and I also don’t think this is a huge focus for them).

    Procore is a system of record — a real platform (moat) — with real cross-selling opportunities.

  2. GTM strategy shift supports growth via new logos + cross-sell (even if upsell is harder).

    Procore’s GTM strategy change aligns with their strategic initiatives and is expected to show results. A general-manager-based GTM strategy allows them to attract new customers as well as cross-sell better. This was seen in Atlassian (to be explored more) and has been successful.

    Upselling is hard for Procore given their premium pricing; however, this new GTM strategy targets cross-selling and new customers, which allows for revenue growth opportunities. With some modest projections, I see revenue above consensus and in the high teens.

  3. Margin expansion optionality.

    Margin compression opportunities: Procore currently only sits in the low teens for Rule of 40. I see margin expansion opportunities with the new GTM strategy (not yet incorporated in the model because I need more research, and I don’t particularly need it right now since a DCF doesn’t work here).

Growth

  • Industry growth: In general, construction is structurally undersupplied.
  • Product/customer expansion: Procore is trying to penetrate specialty contractors.
  • International: ~15% international revenue today → expansion lever.
  • Customer base / stickiness: System of record platform dynamics support retention and expansion.
  • Cross-sell: Real cross-selling opportunities within the existing customer base.

Valuation & Exit Multiple Assumptions

  • Comps: Selected comps based on vertical SaaS software.
  • Multiple: Used a 9.5× 2025E forward EV/Revenue multiple, selected with considerations of the comps set and the multiple chart (see Comps figure below).
  • Context: This is higher than the ~7.5× 2025E multiple implied by current EV.
  • Implied value: This gives a $82.31 share price.

Other Thoughts / Concerns

  • Early-stage posture: The business is still at an early stage where it should be growing fast while also showing strong momentum in cost discipline.
  • Pricing limits: Given pricing is already at a high end, growth through pricing (generally the most effective way to increase revenue) is not a very viable method.
  • Sensitivity: The stock will be highly sensitive to revenue growth rates and cost-cutting initiatives.
  • Margins vs peers: Compared to peers, gross margins are quite high (comparable to INTU), yet GAAP EBITDA margins are still negative — cost-cutting initiatives for opex are another focus in the long term.
